Introduction

Calculus allows us to model continuous change and understand accumulation. From integrals to infinite series, its tools reveal underlying patterns in motion, area, and growth. Consider the classic definition of area under a curve, expressed by the definite integral.

For example, the area under f(x) = x^2 from 0 to 1 is written as an inline integral: \( \int_0^1 x^2 \, dx \).

\[ \int_0^1 x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{3} \]

Infinite Series

Series generalize summation over finite sets to the infinite case. A simple geometric series example is:

\[ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^n = 1 \]

This convergence is foundational in understanding infinite processes in analysis and physics.
