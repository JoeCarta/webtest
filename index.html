Blackbody Radiation and the Planck Integral

Joseph C. – Hunter College, 2025

Introduction

Classical physics failed to predict the spectral distribution of blackbody radiation at high frequencies—leading to the so-called "ultraviolet catastrophe." Planck resolved this by assuming energy quantization, yielding a finite energy distribution integral:

\[ u(\nu, T) = \frac{8\pi h \nu^3}{c^3} \cdot \frac{1}{e^{h\nu / kT} - 1} \]

Integrating over all frequencies gives the total energy density:

\[ U(T) = \int_0^\infty u(\nu, T) \, d\nu = \frac{8\pi^5 k^4}{15 h^3 c^3} T^4 \]

This result is consistent with the Stefan–Boltzmann law and marked the birth of quantum theory.
